Key Technical Projects

Advanced Sintering

High-Density Pure Al MIM (99.5%)

Developed additive-free 99.5% pure aluminum MIM process achieving ≥97% relative density at industrial batch scales (20–160 kg). Significant cost reduction compared to CNC-machined copper.

  • • Kinetic-mechanical oxide disruption via super-solidus liquid phase sintering (SLPS)
  • • Excellent thermal conductivity (~228 W/m·K) and post-process compatibility (anodizing, laser/rod welding)
  • • Applied to high-end camera module cases and high-performance heat dissipation materials for EV/ADAS
Titanium Alloy

High-Density Ti-Fe MIM (without HIP or external hydrogen)

Cost-effective titanium MIM process achieving ≥97% relative density at 24 kg batch scale using TiH₂-Fe blends. Substantial cost reduction versus traditional gas-atomized spherical powders.

  • • In-situ dehydrogenation + transient liquid phase sintering
  • • α+β microstructure with excellent intergranular bonding
  • • Ideal for aerospace, medical, and robotics applications
